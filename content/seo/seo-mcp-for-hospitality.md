# MCP for Hospitality — Every Property, Every System, One Question

You manage a hotel or restaurant group. Your PMS handles reservations. Your POS handles transactions. QuickBooks handles financials. Stripe processes payments. And your booking platform drives revenue.

Five systems. Zero unified view of performance.

## The hospitality data problem

Hotel and restaurant operators toggle between:

- **PMS (Property Management):** Occupancy, ADR, RevPAR, reservations
- **POS:** Sales by category, labor costs, table turns
- **QuickBooks:** P&L by property, vendor payments, payroll
- **Stripe:** Payment processing, deposits, chargebacks
- **Booking platforms:** Channel performance, commission costs

Answering "how did the property perform this weekend?" means checking all five.

## How MCP unifies hospitality ops

Connect your tools. Then ask:

> "What was our RevPAR this week vs last week? Break it down by property."

> "What's our food cost percentage this month? Is it within target?"

> "Show me labor cost as percentage of revenue by property. Flag any over 30%."

> "Which booking channel has the lowest acquisition cost? Highest RevPAR?"

> "What's our group business pipeline for next quarter? Total room nights and revenue?"

## What hospitality operators tell us

> "I used to spend every Monday building a weekend performance report across 4 properties. Now I ask 5 questions and I'm done by 9 AM. I actually get to walk the properties now."
