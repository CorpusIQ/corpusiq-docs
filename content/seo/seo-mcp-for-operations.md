# MCP for Operations Managers — One Question Instead of Twenty Reports

You run operations. That means you touch every system: inventory in Shopify, support tickets in Zendesk, project status in Monday.com, team communication in Slack, financials in QuickBooks.

Five tools. Five logins. Twenty reports. And the CEO still asks "how are we doing?" every morning.

## The ops data trap

Operations managers are the human glue between systems. Your job is to know what's happening across the entire company. But the tools don't talk to each other, so you become the integration layer:

1. Check Shopify for order volume and inventory levels
2. Check Zendesk for ticket volume and response times
3. Check Monday.com for project status and blockers
4. Check Slack for team updates and escalations
5. Check QuickBooks for budget vs actuals
6. Compile everything into a status update

This isn't operations. It's data aggregation. And it burns 2-3 hours every day.

## How MCP makes you the most informed person in the company

Connect every tool once. Then ask one question that spans them all:

> "What happened yesterday? Order volume, support backlog, project status, and any budget variances."

One question. Five data sources. One answer in 15 seconds.

**Daily standup:**
> "What are the top 3 things I need to know this morning across all systems?"

**Weekly ops review:**
> "Show me order volume, support ticket trends, and project completion rate this week vs last week."

**Incident response:**
> "We're seeing a spike in support tickets about shipping delays. Show me related Shopify orders, inventory levels, and any Slack discussions about shipping."

**Vendor management:**
> "Show me all open purchase orders, their status, and when we last communicated with each vendor."

**Team performance:**
> "Which projects are behind schedule? Who's overloaded? Where are the bottlenecks?"
