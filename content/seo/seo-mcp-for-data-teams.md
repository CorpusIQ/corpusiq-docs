# MCP for Data Teams — Stop Being the Company's SQL Help Desk

Your data team built the warehouse. Modeled the data. Maintains the pipelines. And spends 60% of their time answering "can you pull this week's Shopify numbers?"

They didn't sign up to be a help desk. They signed up to do analytics.

## The data team's real job

Data teams exist to: analyze trends, build models, find insights, drive strategy.

What they actually do: "Can you pull revenue by region?" "Can you reconcile Stripe vs QuickBooks?" "Can you update the dashboard with this week's numbers?"

These are data retrieval tasks. Not analysis. Every hour spent pulling data is an hour not spent understanding it.

## How MCP frees your data team

Give operators direct access to live data through AI. They ask their own questions. Your data team focuses on what matters:

> "We built the warehouse. Now let the AI handle the ad-hoc questions. We'll focus on the models that actually move the business."

**Operators ask the AI:**
- "What's revenue this month vs last month?"
- "Which campaigns have the best ROAS?"
- "Show me customer churn by cohort."

**Data team focuses on:**
- Building predictive churn models
- Analyzing cohort retention patterns
- Optimizing pricing and packaging
- Finding growth levers in the data

## The warehouse + MCP stack

The warehouse is for deep analysis. MCP is for day-to-day questions. They're complementary:

| | Warehouse | MCP |
|---|----------|-----|
| **Who uses it** | Data team | Everyone |
| **Questions** | Complex, modeled in advance | Ad-hoc, any question |
| **Freshness** | Scheduled refreshes | Live |
| **Best for** | Board decks, ML, trends | Daily operations, quick answers |
