# MCP for Product Managers — User Data, Revenue, and Roadmap in One Place

You're a PM. Your data lives in: GA4 (user behavior), Stripe (revenue), HubSpot (customer feedback), Jira (engineering velocity), and Slack (team discussions).

Five tools. Zero unified view. And your VP wants the Q3 roadmap justification by Friday.

## The PM data stack

Product managers touch more tools than anyone:

- **GA4 / Mixpanel:** User behavior, feature adoption, retention
- **Stripe:** Revenue, conversion, churn by plan
- **HubSpot:** Customer feedback, feature requests, NPS
- **Jira / Linear:** Engineering velocity, sprint progress
- **Slack:** Team discussions, user complaints, launch announcements

Answering "should we build feature X?" requires data from all five. Currently, that means a week of pulling data and building a case.

## How MCP gives you answers in minutes

Connect your tools. Then ask:

**Feature prioritization:**
> "Which features are customers requesting most? Cross-reference with revenue impact from Stripe."

**Launch impact:**
> "Show me adoption of the new feature — users, retention, and revenue impact since launch."

**User research:**
> "Which users churned this month? What features did they use? What feedback did they give?"

**Roadmap justification:**
> "What's the revenue at risk if we don't fix the top 3 reported bugs?"

**Competitive:**
> "How does our feature adoption compare to the features customers mention in churn feedback?"
