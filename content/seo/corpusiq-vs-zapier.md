# CorpusIQ vs Zapier — Why MCP Changes Everything About Business Automation

**CorpusIQ:** Live data for AI assistants. Ask questions, get real answers from your actual business tools.  
**Zapier:** Workflow automation. "When X happens, do Y."

They solve different problems. Here's why that matters.

## What Zapier does well

Zapier connects apps with workflows. When a new Shopify order comes in, send a Slack message. When a HubSpot deal closes, create a QuickBooks invoice. These are triggers and actions — "if this, then that."

Zapier is great at:
- Automating repetitive tasks across 7,000+ apps
- Moving data between tools on a schedule
- Letting non-technical people build automations
- Handling one-way data flows (trigger → action)

Most operators running a business use Zapier for at least a few workflows. It's reliable, well-documented, and the UI is approachable.

## Where Zapier hits a wall

Zapier moves data from A to B. It doesn't help you understand the data.

You can build a Zap that sends new Stripe charges to a Google Sheet. But you can't ask "which customers account for 80% of our revenue this quarter?" You'd need to open the sheet, pivot the data, and do the math yourself.

Zapier also can't answer cross-tool questions. "Compare this month's Shopify revenue to last month's Meta Ads spend" requires data from two different tools, joined together in real time. Zapier wasn't built for that. It was built to move records one at a time.

## How CorpusIQ is different

CorpusIQ doesn't automate workflows. It connects your tools to an AI assistant that can answer any question you can think of.

Instead of building a workflow for each question, you connect once and ask whatever you want:

- "What's our actual cash position across Stripe and QuickBooks?"
- "Which Klaviyo campaign generated the most Shopify revenue this month?"
- "Show me HubSpot deals closing this week sorted by probability."

Each answer pulls live data from the source tools. No pre-built reports. No "let me export and check."

## The technical difference

| | Zapier | CorpusIQ |
|---|--------|----------|
| **Approach** | Pre-built workflows (triggers + actions) | On-demand queries via AI assistant |
| **Data freshness** | When the Zap runs (every 5-15 min, or on trigger) | Live, every time you ask |
| **Cross-tool queries** | Requires separate Zaps for each combination | One question can span 5+ tools |
| **Setup time** | 5-30 min per workflow | 5 min total (connect once, ask anything) |
| **Maintenance** | Each Zap needs monitoring, debugging, updates | Zero maintenance — tools stay connected |
| **Question flexibility** | Only answers what the Zap was built for | Answers any question you can think of |

## When to use which

**Use Zapier when:**
- You need automated actions (send an email when X happens)
- You need scheduled data movement (sync orders to a sheet daily)
- The task is repetitive and the pattern never changes

**Use CorpusIQ when:**
- You need answers, not automated actions
- Your questions change based on what you learn
- You want to query across multiple tools at once
- You want to stop building reports and just ask the AI

## The reality: they work together

Most operators using CorpusIQ still use Zapier for workflow automation. They're complementary:

Zapier handles the "when X happens, do Y" part of your business. CorpusIQ handles the "what's going on and what should I do about it" part.

Connect your tools to CorpusIQ once. Ask anything. Then use Zapier for the repetitive tasks that don't require thinking.
