# AI for Data Analysis — Stop Exporting, Start Asking

Data analysis used to mean: export CSV, open Excel, build pivot table, create chart, interpret results, present findings. Time: 2-4 hours per question.

AI changes this to: ask question, get answer. Time: 15 seconds.

## The old data analysis workflow

1. Identify the question ("which products have the best margins?")
2. Find where the data lives (QuickBooks? Shopify? Both?)
3. Export CSVs from each source
4. Clean the data (dates don't match, categories are inconsistent)
5. Build analysis in Excel/Python/BI tool
6. Interpret results
7. Present findings

Steps 2-5 are data plumbing. Step 6 is the actual analysis. Most analysts spend 80% of their time on plumbing.

## How AI changes analysis

Connect your data sources via MCP. Then ask:

**Descriptive:** "What happened?"
> "Show me revenue by product category this month."

**Diagnostic:** "Why did it happen?"
> "Why is revenue down in the US? Break it down by customer, product, and channel."

**Predictive:** "What will happen?"
> "Based on current pipeline and historical close rates, what's our likely revenue next quarter?"

**Prescriptive:** "What should we do?"
> "Which customers are at risk of churning? What's the common pattern?"

Each answer: live data, cross-tool, instant.

## What AI can't replace

AI handles the data plumbing. It doesn't replace:

- **Domain expertise:** You know your business. AI doesn't.
- **Strategic judgment:** AI can tell you which customers are at risk. You decide what to do about it.
- **Communication:** AI can produce numbers. You tell the story.

The analyst's job shifts from "pull the data" to "interpret the data and recommend action." That's the part AI can't do. And it's the part that actually matters.
