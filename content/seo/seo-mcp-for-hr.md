# MCP for HR Teams — Headcount, Hiring, and Budget in One Question

HR teams juggle: BambooHR for headcount, Greenhouse for recruiting, QuickBooks for payroll budget, Slack for team communications.

Four tools. Zero unified view of your most expensive asset: people.

## The HR data fragmentation

HR data is scattered across systems that were never designed to talk to each other:

- **BambooHR/Workday:** Headcount, compensation, performance reviews
- **Greenhouse/Lever:** Open roles, pipeline, time-to-hire
- **QuickBooks:** Payroll costs, budget vs actuals
- **Slack/Teams:** Onboarding, offboarding, team announcements
- **HubSpot:** Recruiting agency contacts, offer letter status

Answering "what's our headcount plan vs actual?" means checking three different systems and building a spreadsheet.

## How MCP connects the dots

Connect your HR tools. Then ask:

**Headcount planning:**
> "Show me headcount by department this quarter vs budget. Which teams are over or under?"

**Recruiting:**
> "What's our open headcount, pipeline by role, and average time-to-hire this quarter?"

**Compensation:**
> "What's our total compensation cost by department? Compare to budget."

**Attrition:**
> "What's our voluntary turnover rate this year? Break it down by department and tenure."

**Onboarding:**
> "Show me everyone hired this month. Are they set up in all systems?"

## The read-only guarantee

Every connection is read-only. The AI can query headcount data but cannot modify employee records. It can pull compensation data but cannot change salaries. HR data is sensitive — read-only access is non-negotiable.
