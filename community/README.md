# Community

CorpusIQ development happens in the open. If you want a new connector, hit a
bug, have a question, or just want to see what's coming — this is where that
conversation lives.

Everything runs on [GitHub Discussions](https://github.com/CorpusIQ/corpusiq-docs/discussions)
in this repo. No separate signup, no support portal, no ticket queue. If you
have a GitHub account, you can post.

---

## Where to put what

We use a few categories, and they each do a specific job. Picking the right one
means your post gets seen by the right person faster.

### 💡 Ideas — "I wish CorpusIQ could…"

This is the one to use for early **feature and connector ideas.** Want us to add
a connector for a tool you use? Want a new metric, a new prompt, a different
behavior? Start with an Idea when you want discussion, upvotes, or feedback
before the team scopes the work.

If you already have a specific connector enhancement with a clear use case,
current workaround, and business impact, open a tracked
[Connector Enhancement Request](https://github.com/CorpusIQ/corpusiq-docs/issues/new/choose)
instead. That keeps concrete requests aligned with the issue workflow in
[CONTRIBUTING.md](../CONTRIBUTING.md).

Ideas support **upvotes.** If you see an existing Idea that matches what you
want, upvote it instead of opening a duplicate — that's the single most useful
thing you can do. Upvotes are one of the real inputs into what we build next
(see the [Roadmap](../roadmap/) for how priorities get set).

Before you post: skim the [Roadmap](../roadmap/) first. The thing you want might
already be in flight.

### 🙋 Q&A — "How do I…" / "Why is this…"

Use this for **questions and help.** Setup trouble, a connector behaving
strangely, a prompt that won't return what you expect, "is this supposed to work
this way" — all Q&A.

Q&A posts can be marked with an accepted answer, so the next person with the
same question finds it fast. Before posting, the
[Troubleshooting guide](../troubleshooting/) covers the common cases.

We watch Q&A and answer inbound questions here. If your question is account- or
billing-specific (something you'd rather not post publicly), email
**support@corpusiq.io** instead.

### 📣 Announcements — "Here's what changed"

This is a **read-only, maintainers-only** category. New connectors, notable
releases, and anything you should know about lands here.

You can't post in Announcements, but you can — and should — **subscribe** to it
if you want to know when things ship. It's the lowest-noise way to follow
CorpusIQ. The [Changelog](../changelog/) is the written record of the same
information.

### General, Show and tell, Polls

- **General** — anything that doesn't fit the above. Conversations, feedback,
  "has anyone else…" threads.
- **Show and tell** — built something useful on top of CorpusIQ? A killer
  prompt, a workflow, a dashboard? Share it here. We love these.
- **Polls** — occasionally we'll ask the community to weigh in on a direction
  with a poll. Watch for them.

## A quick map

| You want to…                          | Go to            |
| ------------------------------------- | ---------------- |
| Discuss an early connector or feature idea | **Ideas** 💡 |
| Submit a specific connector enhancement | [Connector Enhancement Request](https://github.com/CorpusIQ/corpusiq-docs/issues/new/choose) |
| Upvote something you also want        | **Ideas** 💡     |
| Ask how to do something / report a bug| **Q&A** 🙋       |
| Find out when something ships         | **Announcements** 📣 (subscribe) |
| Share something you built             | **Show and tell** |
| Something private / billing           | email support@corpusiq.io |

---

## How we handle what you post

We read all of it. Ideas with traction get pulled into roadmap planning. Q&A
gets answered. We can't promise every request gets built — the
[Roadmap](../roadmap/) is honest about what's prioritized, planned, and parked —
but nothing posted here disappears into a void.

Announcements and the public-facing release voice are handled by our team
directly. The build and support conversation — Ideas and Q&A — is where you'll
get the fastest engagement.
